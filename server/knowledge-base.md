## Voter Registration

- **Eligibility**: Indian citizen, 18+ years on the qualifying date (usually January 1st), ordinary resident of the constituency.
- **Form 6**: Application for new voter registration or shifting to a new constituency.
- **Form 7**: Application for objection to / deletion of a name from the electoral roll.
- **Form 8**: Application for correction of entries or shifting within the same constituency.
- **Form 8A**: Application for transposition of entry in the electoral roll (same constituency, different part).
- **Online registration**: Available at https://voters.eci.gov.in and https://www.nvsp.in/
- **Voter Helpline App**: Official mobile app for registration, booth search, and complaints.
- **Qualifying date change (2023)**: The government introduced 4 qualifying dates — January 1, April 1, July 1, and October 1 — so youth turning 18 don't have to wait a full year.

## Valid ID Documents for Voting

The EPIC (Voter ID / Election Photo Identity Card) is the primary document. If unavailable, these 12 alternates are accepted:

1. Aadhaar Card
2. MNREGA Job Card
3. Bank/Post Office Passbook with photograph
4. Health Insurance Smart Card (RSBY)
5. Driving License
6. PAN Card
7. Smart Card issued by RGI under NPR
8. Indian Passport
9. Pension document with photograph
10. Service Identity Card (Govt/PSU employees)
11. Official ID issued by MP/MLA/MLC
12. Unique Disability ID (UDID) Card

## EVM (Electronic Voting Machine)

- First used in 1982 in Paravur, Kerala (by-election).
- Used nationwide since 2004 General Elections.
- Each Ballot Unit can hold 16 candidates. Up to 24 Ballot Units can be connected = max 384 candidates.
- EVMs are **standalone, battery-operated** devices with NO internet/wireless connectivity.
- Manufactured only by BEL (Bharat Electronics Limited) and ECIL (Electronics Corporation of India Limited).
- The blue button next to a candidate's name is pressed to cast the vote.
- A red light glows and a long beep confirms the vote.

## VVPAT (Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail)

- Mandatory in all constituencies since the 2019 General Elections.
- After pressing the EVM button, a paper slip showing the candidate's serial number, name, and symbol is displayed through a glass window for **7 seconds**.
- The slip then drops into a sealed box.
- If a voter claims mismatch, they can request a **Test Vote** under the Presiding Officer's supervision.
- Supreme Court (2019): VVPAT slips of 5 randomly selected polling stations per constituency must be matched with EVM results.

## Model Code of Conduct (MCC)

- Comes into effect **immediately** when the ECI announces the election schedule.
- Not a statutory law, but strictly enforced by the ECI.
- **Key rules**:
  - No government machinery for campaigning.
  - No new schemes/projects after MCC begins.
  - No appeals to caste, religion, or communal sentiments.
  - Public meetings require police permission.
  - Campaigning stops **48 hours before polling** ("Silent Period").
  - Exit polls cannot be published until the last phase is over.
- Violations: Can lead to FIRs, campaign bans, and disqualification.

## Polling Day Procedure

1. Voter arrives at assigned polling station (7 AM to 6 PM generally).
2. Identity verified against electoral roll + ID document.
3. Left index finger marked with indelible ink.
4. Presiding Officer enables the Ballot Unit.
5. Voter enters the compartment, presses the blue button next to their choice.
6. Red light + long beep confirm the vote.
7. VVPAT slip visible for 7 seconds through glass.
8. Slip drops into sealed box. Vote is cast.

## NOTA (None of the Above)

- Available since 2013 (Supreme Court order in PUCL v. Union of India).
- Appears as the last option on the ballot unit.
- Even if NOTA gets the highest votes, the candidate with the next highest votes wins (current rule).

## Voter Rights at the Booth

- **Secrecy**: No one can ask or see who you voted for.
- **No phones/cameras** inside the voting compartment.
- **Tendered Vote (Form 49AA)**: If someone has already voted in your name, you can still cast a tendered ballot.
- **Assistance**: Voters with disabilities or illiteracy can bring a companion.
- **Queue protection**: If you're in line before closing time, you WILL be allowed to vote.
- **Complaints**: Use the cVIGIL app, call 1950 (Voter Helpline), or report to the Returning Officer.

## Key Statistics (2024 General Election)

- Eligible voters: ~968 million (world's largest electorate)

- Lok Sabha seats: 543
- States & UTs: 28 states + 8 Union Territories
- Phases: 7 (April 19 – June 1, 2024)
